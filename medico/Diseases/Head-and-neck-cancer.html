Head and Neck Cancer

Head and neck cancer is a relatively uncommon type of cancer that can develop in more than 30 areas within the head and neck region.

Common areas where head and neck cancer can develop include:

  • Mouth (including the lips)
  • Voice box (larynx)
  • Throat (pharynx)
  • Salivary glands
  • Nose and sinuses
  • Area at the back of the nose and mouth (nasopharynx)

Note: Oesophageal (gullet) cancer, thyroid cancer, brain tumours and eye cancer don't tend to be classified as head and neck cancer.

Mouth Cancer

Mouth cancer is the most common type of head and neck cancer. It can affect various areas in and around the mouth.

Affected Areas

Mouth cancer can develop in:

  • Lips
  • Tongue
  • Inside of the cheeks
  • Floor or roof of the mouth
  • Gums

Symptoms

Common symptoms of mouth cancer include:

  • Persistent mouth ulcers
  • A lump in your mouth
  • Pain in the affected area

Early detection: Regular dental check-ups can help detect mouth cancer early. Any persistent mouth ulcers or lumps should be examined by a healthcare professional.

Laryngeal Cancer

Laryngeal cancer develops in the tissue of the larynx (voice box), which plays a crucial role in breathing, swallowing, and speaking.

Symptoms

Symptoms of laryngeal cancer can include:

  • A change in the voice, such as persistent hoarseness
  • Difficulty or pain when swallowing
  • Noisy breathing
  • Shortness of breath
  • A persistent cough
  • A lump or swelling in your neck

Important: Persistent hoarseness lasting more than 3 weeks should always be evaluated by a doctor, especially in smokers or heavy drinkers.

Throat Cancers

The term "throat cancer" encompasses several different types of cancer that affect various parts of the throat (pharynx).

Main Areas Affected

  • Oropharynx – the part of the throat at the back of the mouth
  • Hypopharynx – the part connecting the oropharynx to the gullet and windpipe
  • Nasopharynx – the part connecting the back of the nose to the back of the mouth

Common Symptoms

The most common symptoms of cancer in the oropharynx or hypopharynx include:

  • A lump in the neck
  • A persistent sore throat
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Ear pain
  • Unexplained weight loss

Salivary Gland Cancer

Salivary glands produce saliva, which keeps your mouth moist and helps with swallowing and digestion.

Main Salivary Glands

  • Parotid glands – located between your cheeks and your ears
  • Sublingual glands – located under your tongue
  • Submandibular glands – located under each side of your jawbone

Symptoms

The main symptom of salivary gland cancer is a lump or swelling on or near your jaw, or in your mouth or neck.

Other symptoms can include:

  • Numbness in part of your face
  • Drooping on one side of your face
  • Persistent pain in the salivary gland area
  • Difficulty swallowing or opening your mouth widely

Important note: The vast majority of lumps in the salivary glands are non-cancerous, but any persistent lump should be evaluated by a doctor.

Nose and Sinus Cancer

Nose and sinus cancer affects the nasal cavity (above the roof of your mouth) and the sinuses (small, air-filled cavities inside the bones of the nose and within the cheekbones and forehead).

Symptoms

Symptoms of nose and sinus cancer are similar to viral or bacterial infections but persist longer:

  • A persistent blocked nose, usually only affecting one side
  • Nosebleeds
  • A decreased sense of smell
  • Mucus running from the nose or down the throat
  • Pain or pressure in the sinuses
  • Loosening or numbness of teeth

When to see a doctor: Symptoms that persist for more than a few weeks, especially if they only affect one side of the nose or face, should be evaluated by a healthcare professional.

Nasopharyngeal Cancer

Nasopharyngeal cancer affects the part of the throat that connects the back of the nose to the back of the mouth. It's one of the rarest types of head and neck cancer in the UK.

Symptoms

Symptoms can include:

  • A lump in the neck, due to the cancer spreading to the lymph nodes
  • A blocked or stuffy nose
  • Nosebleeds
  • Hearing loss (usually only in one ear)
  • Tinnitus (ringing in the ears)
  • Headaches
  • Double vision

Medical attention needed: Any persistent lump in the neck, especially if accompanied by other symptoms like hearing loss or nasal obstruction, should be evaluated promptly by a doctor.

Risk Factors

Certain factors may increase the risk of developing nasopharyngeal cancer:

  • Epstein-Barr virus infection
  • Family history of the disease
  • Certain dietary factors (high consumption of salt-cured foods)
  • Asian ancestry, particularly Southern Chinese
