Liver Disease

There are more than 100 different types of liver disease, which together affect at least 2 million people in the UK.

Liver disease doesn't usually cause any obvious signs or symptoms until it's fairly advanced and the liver is damaged.

At this stage, possible symptoms can include loss of appetite, weight loss and jaundice.

Early detection: Regular check-ups and maintaining a healthy lifestyle can help detect liver problems early when they're most treatable.

The Liver and Its Functions

The liver is the second largest organ in the body. It works hard, performing hundreds of complex functions that are essential for life.

Key Functions of the Liver

  • Fighting infections and illness - The liver plays a crucial role in your immune system
  • Removing toxins - Filters harmful substances like alcohol from the body
  • Controlling cholesterol levels - Helps maintain healthy cholesterol balance
  • Blood clotting - Produces proteins that help blood to clot properly
  • Digestion - Releases bile that breaks down fats and aids digestion
  • Metabolism - Processes nutrients from food and converts them into energy
  • Storage - Stores vitamins, minerals, and sugars for when your body needs them

Important: The liver is a remarkable organ that can regenerate itself, but chronic damage can lead to permanent scarring (cirrhosis).

Types of Liver Disease

Liver disease encompasses a wide range of conditions that affect liver function. Here are some common types:

Common Liver Conditions

  • Alcohol-related liver disease - Liver damage caused by years of alcohol misuse, which can lead to cirrhosis
  • Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease - Build-up of fat in liver cells, commonly seen in overweight or obese individuals
  • Hepatitis - Inflammation of the liver caused by viral infections or exposure to harmful substances
  • Haemochromatosis - Inherited disorder causing gradual iron build-up in the body, primarily affecting the liver
  • Primary biliary cirrhosis - Rare, long-term disease that damages the bile ducts in the liver
  • Liver cancer - Can be primary (starting in the liver) or secondary (spread from elsewhere)
  • Autoimmune hepatitis - When the body's immune system attacks liver cells

All types of liver disease can cause cirrhosis (scarring of the liver), not just alcohol-related liver disease.

Warning: Cirrhosis is often irreversible and can lead to liver failure if not properly managed.

Prevention and Healthy Liver Tips

In the UK, liver disease is on the increase. The 3 main causes of liver disease are all preventable:

  • Obesity - Leading to non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
  • Undiagnosed hepatitis infection - Often preventable with vaccination and safe practices
  • Alcohol misuse - One of the most common causes of liver damage

Do's for Liver Health

  • Maintain a healthy weight for your height
  • Avoid drinking excessive amounts of alcohol
  • Get vaccinated against hepatitis if you're at risk
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables
  • Exercise regularly to maintain healthy weight
  • Practice safe sex to prevent hepatitis transmission
  • Use medications responsibly and follow dosage instructions
  • Get regular health check-ups, especially if you have risk factors

Remember: Your liver is incredibly resilient. By adopting healthy lifestyle habits, you can significantly reduce your risk of liver disease and maintain optimal liver function throughout your life.
