About Sepsis

Sepsis is a serious complication of an infection. Without quick treatment, sepsis can lead to multiple organ failure and death.

Emergency: Phone 999 immediately or go to A&E if you or someone else has symptoms like loss of consciousness, severe breathlessness, high fever, confusion, slurred speech, cold clammy skin, fast heartbeat, or fast breathing.

What Causes Sepsis?

The immune system usually keeps an infection limited to one place (localised infection). However, an infection can spread if the immune system is weak or the infection is severe, leading to widespread inflammation that can damage tissue and interfere with blood flow.

Common Sources of Infection

  • Lungs
  • Urinary tract
  • Abdomen (tummy)
  • Pelvis

Infections Linked to Sepsis

  • Appendicitis
  • Cellulitis (skin infection)
  • Pneumonia (lung infection)
  • Meningitis
  • Urinary tract infections
  • Post-surgery infections
  • Endocarditis (heart infection)

Who's at Risk?

Anyone can develop sepsis, but some people are more vulnerable:

  • Very young or very old people
  • Pregnant women
  • People with long-term health conditions like diabetes
  • People with weakened immune systems
  • Recent surgery patients
  • People already in hospital with serious illness

Diagnosing Sepsis

Sepsis is often diagnosed by testing temperature, heart rate, breathing rate, and blood. Other tests include urine/stool samples, wound cultures, and imaging studies like X-rays or CT scans.

Symptoms of Sepsis

Sepsis Symptoms in Children Under 5

Temperature

  • Over 38°C (babies under 3 months)
  • Over 39°C (babies aged 3 to 6 months)
  • Below 36°C

Breathing

  • Difficulty breathing
  • Grunting with every breath
  • Struggling to speak

Body Symptoms

  • Bulging soft spot on baby's head
  • Sunken eyes
  • Floppiness
  • Weak, continuous crying
  • Not urinating for 12 hours

Emergency for Children: Go to A&E or call 999 if your child is very lethargic, feels abnormally cold, is breathing very fast, has a rash that doesn't fade when pressed, or has a fit.

Sepsis Symptoms in Older Children and Adults

Early symptoms may include:

  • High temperature (fever) or low body temperature
  • Chills and shivering
  • Fast heartbeat
  • Fast breathing

Severe Sepsis or Septic Shock Symptoms

  • Feeling dizzy or faint
  • Confusion or disorientation
  • Diarrhoea
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Slurred speech
  • Severe muscle pain
  • Severe breathlessness
  • Cold, clammy and pale skin
  • Loss of consciousness

Important: Phone 999 or go to A&E if you've recently had an infection or injury and have possible signs of sepsis. Don't be afraid to ask healthcare professionals "is this sepsis?"

Treating Sepsis

Treatment for sepsis varies depending on the area affected, cause of infection, organs affected, and extent of damage.

Emergency Treatment

You'll need emergency treatment or treatment in an intensive care unit (ICU) if sepsis is severe or if you develop septic shock (dangerously low blood pressure).

Sepsis is treatable if identified and treated quickly. In most cases it leads to full recovery with no lasting problems.

Antibiotics

The main treatment for sepsis is antibiotics given directly into a vein (intravenously). Ideally, antibiotic treatment should start within an hour of diagnosis.

Broad-spectrum antibiotics are given first, then more focused antibiotics once the specific bacterium is identified.

Other Treatments

  • Intravenous fluids - To prevent dehydration and kidney failure
  • Oxygen therapy - If blood oxygen levels are low
  • Source control - Treating the original infection source
  • Blood pressure medications - Vasopressors for low blood pressure
  • Additional support - Corticosteroids, insulin, blood transfusions, mechanical ventilation, or dialysis

Recovery: Recovery time varies depending on sepsis severity, overall health, hospital stay duration, and whether ICU treatment was needed. Some people experience long-term effects known as post-sepsis syndrome.

Long-term Effects

Some people experience post-sepsis syndrome with symptoms like:

  • Lethargy or excessive tiredness
  • Muscle weakness
  • Swollen limbs
  • Joint pain
  • Chest pain
  • Breathlessness
