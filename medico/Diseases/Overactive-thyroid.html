About Overactive Thyroid

Overactive thyroid (also known as hyperthyroidism) is a relatively common hormonal condition that occurs when there is too much thyroid hormone in the body.

What is Hyperthyroidism?

Excess levels of thyroid hormones can speed up the body's metabolism, triggering a range of symptoms. The thyroid gland is found in the neck and produces hormones that control the body's growth and metabolism.

Prevalence: Women are 10 times more likely to have an overactive thyroid gland than men. In most cases, symptoms begin between ages 20 and 40.

Common Symptoms Include:

  • Nervousness and anxiety
  • Hyperactivity and restlessness
  • Unexplained or unplanned weight loss
  • Swelling of the thyroid gland (goitre)
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Increased sweating
  • Sleep difficulties

Symptoms of Overactive Thyroid

An overactive thyroid (hyperthyroidism) has many signs and symptoms, although it is unlikely you would develop all of them.

Common Symptoms

  • Hyperactivity - inability to stay still
  • Mood swings - anxiety, irritability, nervousness
  • Sleep problems - difficulty sleeping (insomnia)
  • Fatigue - feeling tired all the time
  • Muscle weakness
  • Increased bowel movements
  • Sensitivity to heat and excessive sweating
  • Unexplained weight loss despite increased appetite
  • Menstrual changes - very infrequent or light periods
  • Infertility and loss of interest in sex

Physical Signs

  • Swelling in neck (goitre)
  • Irregular and/or unusually fast heart rate
  • Trembling or shaking (tremor)
  • Warm, moist skin
  • Redness on palms of hands
  • Patchy hair loss
  • Twitching in face and limbs

Causes of Overactive Thyroid

Overactive thyroid occurs when your thyroid gland produces too much of the thyroid hormones thyroxine or triiodothyronine.

Graves' Disease

This is the most common cause of overactive thyroid. It's an autoimmune condition where the immune system attacks the thyroid gland, leading to overproduction of thyroid hormones.

Thyroid Nodules

Lumps that develop in the thyroid gland can contain abnormal thyroid tissue that affects normal hormone production, causing hyperthyroidism.

Other Causes

  • Iodine supplements - can cause overproduction of thyroid hormones
  • Amiodarone medication - used for irregular heartbeat
  • Thyroid cancer - in rare cases
  • Thyroiditis - inflammation of the thyroid gland

Risk factors: Graves' disease can run in families and is more common in women aged 20-40. Smoking increases the risk of developing Graves' disease.

Diagnosing Overactive Thyroid

A diagnosis will be based on your symptoms and the results of blood tests that assess how well your thyroid gland is working.

Thyroid Function Tests

Your GP will test your blood for levels of:

  • Thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) - usually low in hyperthyroidism
  • Thyroxine and triiodothyronine - usually high in hyperthyroidism

Additional Tests

If tests confirm an overactive thyroid, you may need further tests to determine the underlying cause:

  • Isotope thyroid scan - measures iodine absorption
  • Antibody tests - to check for autoimmune conditions
  • Ultrasound scan - to examine thyroid structure

Referral: If diagnosed with hyperthyroidism, you'll be referred to an endocrinologist (hormone specialist) for further management and treatment planning.

Treating Overactive Thyroid

An overactive thyroid usually responds well to treatment, and most people are able to control their symptoms effectively.

Thionamides

Medications like carbimazole and propylthiouracil that stop the thyroid gland producing excess hormones.

  • Usually taken for several weeks before improvement
  • May need long-term treatment
  • Can cause side effects in some people

Radioiodine Treatment

A form of radiotherapy that shrinks the thyroid gland, reducing hormone production.

  • Very effective treatment
  • Given as a drink or capsule
  • Not suitable during pregnancy

Surgery

Removal of all or part of the thyroid gland (thyroidectomy).

  • Permanent cure for recurrent hyperthyroidism
  • Used for large goitres or when other treatments unsuitable
  • Requires lifelong thyroid hormone replacement

Beta-blockers

Can relieve symptoms like rapid heartbeat and tremor while waiting for other treatments to take effect.

Treatment success: Most people with hyperthyroidism respond well to treatment and can manage their condition effectively with proper medical care.

Complications of Overactive Thyroid

Several complications can occur with an overactive thyroid, particularly if the condition is not treated.

Graves' Ophthalmopathy

Eye problems affecting about 1 in 20 people with Graves' disease:

  • Dry, gritty eyes
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Double vision
  • Bulging eyes
  • Vision loss in severe cases

Thyroid Storm

Thyroid storm is a rare but life-threatening complication requiring immediate medical attention.

  • Very rapid heartbeat (over 140 bpm)
  • High fever
  • Severe dehydration with vomiting/diarrhoea
  • Confusion and agitation
  • Hallucinations

If you suspect a thyroid storm, phone 999 immediately.

Other Complications

  • Underactive thyroid - can result from treatment
  • Pregnancy complications - increased risk of miscarriage and premature birth
  • Heart problems - irregular heartbeat and heart failure
  • Osteoporosis - weakened bones

Important: Regular follow-up with your healthcare team is essential to monitor for complications and adjust treatment as needed.
