Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD) is a mental health condition. It causes a person to have obsessive thoughts and carry out compulsive activity.

This can be distressing and can have a big impact on your life. Treatment can help you keep it under control.

Important: OCD is a treatable condition, and many people successfully manage their symptoms with proper treatment and support.

Symptoms of OCD

OCD affects people differently. It usually causes a particular pattern of thought and behaviour.

Obsessive Thoughts

Almost everyone has unpleasant or unwanted thoughts at some point in their life. Most people are able to put these types of thoughts and concerns into context and carry on with their day-to-day life.

If you have persistent and unwanted thoughts that dominate your thinking, you may have developed an obsession.

Compulsive Behaviour

Compulsions happen as a way of trying to reduce or prevent the harm of the obsessive thought. However, this behaviour is either excessive or not connected at all.

For example, a person who fears becoming contaminated with dirt and germs may wash their hands repeatedly. Or someone with a fear of causing harm to their family may have the urge to repeat an action multiple times.

Understanding: Most people with OCD realise that such compulsive behaviour is irrational. They might know it makes no logical sense, but they cannot stop acting on their compulsion.

Related Problems

Some people with OCD may also have or develop other mental health problems, including:

  • Depression
  • Eating disorders
  • Generalised anxiety disorder
  • Hoarding disorder

Important: People with OCD and severe depression might also have suicidal feelings. If you're experiencing these thoughts, please seek immediate help.

Causes of OCD

The exact cause of OCD is not known. There are some factors that could make a person more likely to have OCD.

These factors might include:

  • Genetics
  • Life events
  • Personality traits
  • Chemical differences in the brain

Diagnosis and treatment can help to reduce the impact of OCD on your day-to-day life.

Treating OCD

Treatment for obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD) depends on how much the condition is affecting your daily life.
