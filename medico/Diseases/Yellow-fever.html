Yellow Fever

Yellow fever is a serious viral infection that is spread by certain types of mosquito. It's mainly found in sub-Saharan Africa, South America and parts of the Caribbean.

The condition can be prevented with a vaccination and is a very rare cause of illness in travellers.

Between 1999 and 2018, there were 12 cases of yellow fever reported among European travellers. None of these travellers were vaccinated, and 5 of these cases occurred in 2018.

Travel Advisory: If you're planning to visit places where yellow fever infection is found, you should seek travel health advice before you travel. Check current risk areas before planning your trip.

Symptoms of Yellow Fever

The symptoms of yellow fever occur in 2 stages. The initial symptoms develop 3 to 6 days after infection.

Initial Stage Symptoms

The first stage typically includes:

  • A high temperature (fever)
  • Headache
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Muscle pain, including backache
  • Loss of appetite

This stage will usually pass after 3 to 4 days and most people will make a full recovery.

Severe Stage Symptoms

However, around 15% of people go on to develop more serious problems within 24 hours of apparent recovery:

  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes)
  • Kidney failure
  • Bleeding from the mouth, nose, eyes or stomach
  • Blood in vomit and stools

Critical Warning: Up to half of those who experience these severe symptoms will die. Immediate medical attention is crucial.

ðŸš¨ When to Seek Medical Advice

You should see a doctor as soon as possible if you develop symptoms of yellow fever and:

  • Are currently travelling in an area where the virus is found
  • Have recently returned from one of these areas

To help determine whether you have yellow fever, the doctor will want to know exactly where you have been travelling and what symptoms you have. A blood test will be needed to confirm the diagnosis.

How Yellow Fever is Spread

The virus that causes yellow fever is passed to humans through the bites of infected mosquitoes.

Mosquito Characteristics

  • The mosquitoes that spread yellow fever are usually active and bite during daylight hours
  • They are found in both urban and rural areas
  • They belong to the Aedes and Haemagogus species

Important: Yellow fever can't be passed directly from person to person through close contact. It requires mosquito transmission.

High-Risk Areas

Yellow fever is mainly found in:

  • Sub-Saharan Africa: Many countries in this region
  • South America: Particularly tropical regions
  • Parts of the Caribbean: Certain islands

Treating Yellow Fever

No Specific Treatment: There is no specific antiviral treatment for yellow fever. Medical care focuses on managing symptoms and supporting the body's immune response.

Hospital Care

If diagnosed with yellow fever, you will be admitted to hospital for:

  • Close monitoring of vital signs
  • Management of fever and pain
  • Fluid replacement to prevent dehydration
  • Treatment of bleeding complications
  • Dialysis for kidney failure if necessary
  • Respiratory support if needed

Recovery

Most people who survive yellow fever make a full recovery and develop lifelong immunity to the virus. However, recovery can be slow, taking several weeks or months.

Preventing Yellow Fever

Vaccination

The yellow fever vaccine is the most important preventive measure against yellow fever.

ðŸ’‰ Vaccine Protection

The yellow fever vaccine protects you from infection when you travel to a country where yellow fever virus occurs and prevents the disease from spreading between countries.

Vaccine Certificate

After you have received the vaccine, you are given a yellow fever vaccine certificate:

  • This certificate proves you have been vaccinated and are protected
  • It's designed to fit inside your passport
  • The certificate is valid for life
  • Some countries require proof of vaccination for entry

Mosquito Protection

In addition to vaccination, protect yourself from mosquito bites:

  • Use insect repellent containing DEET, picaridin, or oil of lemon eucalyptus
  • Wear long-sleeved shirts and long pants
  • Stay in accommodations with air conditioning or window/door screens
  • Use mosquito nets if sleeping outdoors
  • Avoid outdoor activities during peak mosquito biting hours

Travel Planning: If you're traveling to areas where yellow fever is present, get vaccinated at least 10 days before your trip. Some countries may deny entry without a valid yellow fever vaccination certificate.
