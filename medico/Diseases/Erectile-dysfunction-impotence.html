About Erectile Dysfunction

Erectile dysfunction (ED), also known as impotence, is the inability to get and maintain an erection.

Erectile dysfunction is a very common condition, particularly in older men. It is estimated that half of all men between the ages of 40 and 70 will have it to some degree.

When to See Your GP

See your GP if you have erectile dysfunction for more than a few weeks. They will assess your general state of health because the condition can be the first sign of more serious health conditions, such as heart disease.

Important: ED can be an early warning sign of cardiovascular disease. Always consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis.

Why Does Erectile Dysfunction Happen?

Erectile dysfunction can have a range of causes, both physical and psychological.

Physical causes include:

  • Narrowing of blood vessels to the penis
  • Hormonal problems
  • Surgery or injury
  • Neurological conditions

Psychological causes include:

  • Anxiety and stress
  • Depression
  • Relationship problems
  • Performance anxiety

Symptoms of Erectile Dysfunction

The main symptom of erectile dysfunction is the inability to get and maintain an erection for satisfactory intercourse.

Note: ED should not be confused with ejaculation problems such as premature ejaculation.

Types of Symptoms

  • Consistent ED: Unable to get an erection under any circumstances
  • Situational ED: Can get erections in some situations but not others
  • Reduced sexual desire: Often accompanying erection problems

Warning: Many websites offer treatments for ED but their use is not recommended. The medications offered could be fake and dangerous. Only take medication prescribed by your GP.

Causes of Erectile Dysfunction

ED can have many causes, including medical conditions, medications, and psychological factors.

Physical Causes

  • Cardiovascular disease - affects blood flow to penis
  • Diabetes - can damage blood vessels and nerves
  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • Obesity
  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Neurological conditions

Medication Causes

Some medications that can cause ED include:

  • Antidepressants
  • Blood pressure medications
  • Diuretics
  • Antihistamines
  • Prostate cancer treatments

Psychological Causes

  • Depression and anxiety
  • Stress
  • Relationship problems
  • Performance anxiety
  • Past sexual trauma

Lifestyle Factors

  • Smoking
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Drug use
  • Lack of exercise
  • Poor diet

Diagnosing Erectile Dysfunction

Erectile dysfunction can often be diagnosed by your GP through discussion and physical examination.

What to Expect

Your GP may ask about:

  • Your symptoms and medical history
  • Your sexual history and relationships
  • Medications you're taking
  • Lifestyle factors (smoking, alcohol, exercise)
  • Psychological factors

Physical Examination

  • Blood pressure measurement
  • Heart and circulation assessment
  • Examination of genitals
  • Neurological examination

Tests That May Be Conducted

  • Blood tests (testosterone, blood sugar, cholesterol)
  • Urine tests
  • Ultrasound of blood vessels
  • Psychological assessment

Remember: Being open and honest with your healthcare provider is crucial for accurate diagnosis and effective treatment.

Treating Erectile Dysfunction

Treatment for ED depends on the underlying cause and may involve lifestyle changes, medication, therapy, or a combination of approaches.

Lifestyle Changes

  • Exercise regularly - improves blood flow
  • Healthy diet - maintains cardiovascular health
  • Weight loss - if overweight
  • Quit smoking - improves circulation
  • Reduce alcohol - excessive drinking can cause ED
  • Stress management - through relaxation techniques

Medications

Common ED medications include:

  • Sildenafil (Viagra) - works for 4-6 hours
  • Tadalafil (Cialis) - works for up to 36 hours
  • Vardenafil (Levitra) - works for 4-5 hours
  • Avanafil (Spedra) - works quickly, within 15 minutes

Important: Never take ED medications without prescription. They can interact dangerously with other medications, especially nitrates used for heart conditions.

Other Treatments

  • Psychological counseling - for anxiety or relationship issues
  • Vacuum erection devices - mechanical pumps
  • Penile injections - medication injected into the penis
  • Testosterone replacement - if low testosterone is the cause
  • Surgery - penile implants or vascular surgery

Alternative Approaches

  • Pelvic floor exercises
  • Acupuncture (limited evidence)
  • Herbal supplements (use with caution)

Good to know: Most cases of ED are treatable. With the right approach, the majority of men can regain satisfactory sexual function.
