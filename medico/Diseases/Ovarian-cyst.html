Ovarian Cyst Information

About Ovarian Cysts Symptoms Diagnosis Treatment

About Ovarian Cysts

An ovarian cyst is a fluid-filled sac that develops on an ovary. They're very common and do not usually cause any symptoms.

Most ovarian cysts are generally harmless and disappear without treatment in a few months. Small cysts are normal in the ovary.

What are Ovaries?

The ovaries are part of the female reproductive system - two small, bean-shaped organs, one on each side of the womb (uterus). Their main functions are:

  • To release an egg around every 28 days as part of the menstrual cycle
  • To release the female sex hormones, oestrogen and progesterone

Types of Ovarian Cysts

  • Functional ovarian cysts - Most common, develop as part of the menstrual cycle, usually harmless and short-lived
  • Pathological ovarian cysts - Much less common, occur due to abnormal cell growth

Note: The vast majority of ovarian cysts are non-cancerous (benign). Ovarian cysts can sometimes be caused by underlying conditions like endometriosis.

Symptoms of Ovarian Cysts

Most cysts are found by chance during a scan and are unlikely to cause symptoms unless they're large or abnormal.

When to Seek Medical Attention

Urgent Care: Contact your GP immediately if you have sudden, severe pelvic pain. If your GP is closed, phone 111.

Contact your GP if you regularly experience:

  • Pelvic pain - from dull, heavy sensation to sudden, severe sharp pain
  • Pain during sex
  • Difficulty having bowel movements
  • Frequent need to urinate
  • Heavy periods, irregular periods, or lighter periods than normal
  • Bloating and swollen tummy
  • Feeling very full after eating only a little
  • Difficulty getting pregnant

Useful Information for Your Doctor

Keep track of your symptoms and be prepared to discuss:

  • First day of your last period
  • Duration of your periods
  • Shortest and longest time between periods
  • Heaviness of flow
  • Date of last smear test (if over 25)

Diagnosing Ovarian Cysts

If your GP suspects an ovarian cyst, you'll likely be referred for further investigation.

Diagnostic Process

  • Ultrasound scan - Usually performed with a probe placed inside the vagina
  • Blood tests - To check for other conditions
  • Follow-up monitoring - Repeat ultrasound scans if cyst is identified
  • Gynaecology referral - To a specialist if needed

Note: Vaginal ultrasound examinations are intimate procedures. You can request a female doctor and will have a chaperone present during the examination.

Treating Ovarian Cysts

In most cases, ovarian cysts disappear within a few months without treatment. Treatment depends on various factors.

Important: Most ovarian cysts do not need treatment or surgery.

Treatment Considerations

  • Size and appearance of the cysts
  • Presence of symptoms
  • Whether you're post-menopausal (higher risk of ovarian cancer)

Surgical Options

Large cysts, symptomatic cysts, or those that could be cancerous may require surgical removal.

Laparoscopy (Keyhole Surgery)

  • Small cuts in tummy with gas blown into pelvis
  • Camera inserted to view internal organs
  • Cyst removed through small skin cuts
  • Less pain, quicker recovery (usually home same/next day)

Laparotomy

  • Single larger cut for better access
  • Used for very large or potentially cancerous cysts
  • May involve removing entire cyst and ovary
  • Longer recovery (several days in hospital)

Recovery After Surgery

  • Laparoscopy: 2 weeks recovery time
  • Laparotomy: 6-8 weeks recovery time
  • Watch for signs of infection (heavy bleeding, severe pain, fever, smelly discharge)

Fertility Considerations

  • Surgeons aim to preserve reproductive system when possible
  • Often possible to remove only the cyst
  • If one ovary removed, fertility largely unaffected
  • If both ovaries removed, early menopause occurs
  • Discuss fertility concerns with surgeon before operation
