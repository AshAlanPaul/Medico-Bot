About Sore Throat

Sore throats are very common and usually nothing to worry about. They normally get better within a week.

Most are caused by minor illnesses such as cold or flu and can be treated at home.

Note: Sore throats are usually not serious and most cases resolve on their own with proper self-care.

Treatments for a Sore Throat

There are things you can do to help soothe a sore throat and speed up recovery.

Do

  • Take ibuprofen or paracetamol (paracetamol is better for children)
  • Drink plenty of cool or warm fluids
  • Eat cool, soft foods
  • Avoid smoking and smoky places
  • Suck lozenges, hard sweets, ice cubes or ice lollies
  • Gargle with warm, salty water (adults only)

Important: Antibiotics aren't usually prescribed for sore throats, even if caused by bacterial infection. They're unlikely to make you feel better quicker and have unpleasant side effects.

When to Get Professional Advice

Go to A&E or phone 999 if:

You or your child have:

  • Symptoms that are severe or getting worse quickly
  • Difficulty breathing or swallowing
  • Severe pain
  • Started drooling
  • A muffled voice
  • A high-pitched sound as you breathe (stridor)

When to See a Pharmacist or GP

You should seek medical advice if:

  • Your symptoms are severe or getting worse
  • You have persistent symptoms that haven't improved after a week
  • You experience severe sore throats frequently
  • You have a weak immune system
  • You have a persistent sore throat (lasting 3-4 weeks)

Pharmacy services: You can get advice and treatment directly from a pharmacy for sore throat symptoms.

Causes of a Sore Throat

The cause of a sore throat isn't always obvious, but in most cases it's a symptom of a viral or bacterial infection.

Common Causes

  • Colds or flu: Often accompanied by runny nose, cough, fever, and headache
  • Laryngitis: Inflammation of the voice box, may cause hoarse voice
  • Tonsillitis: Inflammation of the tonsils, may cause red tonsils and fever
  • Strep throat: Bacterial infection with swollen glands
  • Glandular fever: Causes fatigue, fever, and swollen glands

Other Causes

Sore throat can also be caused by:

  • Smoke or pollution
  • Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease (acid reflux)
  • Allergies
  • Dry air

Less Common but Serious Causes

  • Quinsy: Painful collection of pus at back of throat
  • Epiglottitis: Inflammation of the epiglottis (requires emergency care)

Emergency warning: Quinsy and epiglottitis are serious conditions that require immediate medical attention.
