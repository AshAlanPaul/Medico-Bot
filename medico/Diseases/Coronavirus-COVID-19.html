Coronavirus (COVID-19)

Coronavirus, and other respiratory infections such as flu, can spread easily and cause serious illness in some people. Vaccinations are very effective at preventing serious illness from coronavirus.

Testing: Most people no longer need to take a coronavirus test. To prevent the spread of infection, you should try to stay at home if you're unwell.

Symptoms

To prevent the spread of coronavirus, try to stay at home and avoid contact with other people if you have symptoms of a respiratory infection such as coronavirus and you:

  • Have a high temperature, or
  • Do not feel well enough to go to work or carry out normal activities

Common COVID-19 Symptoms

  • Continuous cough
  • High temperature, fever or chills
  • Loss of, or change in, your normal sense of taste or smell
  • Shortness of breath
  • Unexplained tiredness, lack of energy
  • Muscle aches or pains that are not due to exercise
  • Not wanting to eat or not feeling hungry
  • Headache that's unusual or longer lasting than usual
  • Sore throat, stuffy or runny nose
  • Diarrhoea
  • Feeling sick or being sick

Self-Care for COVID-19 Symptoms

Do

  • Drink fluids like water to keep yourself hydrated
  • Get plenty of rest
  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing - don't try to make yourself too cold
  • Take over-the-counter medications like paracetamol - always follow the manufacturer's instructions

Important: Antibiotics will not relieve your symptoms or speed up your recovery from COVID-19.

You might continue to have a cough or feel tired after your other symptoms have improved. This does not mean that you're still infectious.

When to Seek Emergency Help

Phone 999 or go to A&E if:

  • You or your child seems very unwell, is getting worse or you think there's something seriously wrong
  • Gets sudden chest pain
  • Is so breathless they're unable to say short sentences when resting
  • Starts coughing up blood
  • Collapses, faints, or has a seizure or fit for the first time
  • Has a rash that does not fade when you roll a glass over it (meningitis sign)

Speak to your GP if:

  • Your symptoms worsen
  • You're concerned about your symptoms
  • You have symptoms that you can no longer manage at home
  • You're worried about your child, especially if they're under 2 years

If your GP is closed, phone 111. In an emergency phone 999.

Higher Risk Groups: It's particularly important to get help if you're pregnant, aged 60 or over, or have a weakened immune system.

Stay at Home Advice

If You Have Symptoms

If you have symptoms of a respiratory infection and have a high temperature or do not feel well enough to go to work or carry out normal activities, try to stay at home and avoid contact with other people.

If You Test Positive

If you have a positive coronavirus test result, try to stay at home and avoid contact with other people for 5 days after the day you took your test, or from the day your symptoms started (whichever was earlier).

For Children and Young People

Children and young people aged 18 or under should try to stay at home for 3 days after a positive test or from when symptoms started.

After Isolation: Although many people will no longer be infectious after 5 days, some people may be infectious for up to 10 days. Continue to be cautious around vulnerable people.

How to Reduce the Spread

Do

  • Work from home if you can
  • Tell medical appointments about your symptoms or positive test
  • Ask others to get essentials for you
  • Inform people you've been in contact with
  • Keep your distance from people you live with
  • Ventilate rooms by opening windows
  • Wear a well-fitting face covering if you leave home
  • Regularly clean frequently touched surfaces
  • Exercise outdoors away from others
  • Cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing
  • Wash hands regularly with soap and water

Don't

  • Do not have close contact with anyone at higher risk
  • Do not go to crowded or poorly ventilated places
  • Do not touch your face with unwashed hands

Children and Young People

Respiratory infections are common in children and young people, particularly during winter months. For most, these illnesses will not be serious and they'll soon recover with rest and fluids.

When Children Should Stay Home

Children with mild symptoms (runny nose, sore throat, slight cough) who are otherwise well can continue to attend school.

Children who are unwell and have a high temperature should stay at home until they no longer have a high temperature and are well enough to attend.

Testing: It's not recommended that children and young people are tested for coronavirus unless advised by a healthcare professional.
