Angina

Angina is chest pain which occurs when the blood supply to the heart becomes restricted because of narrowing or blockages in the blood vessels around the heart.

Symptoms of Angina

The main symptom of angina is chest pain. This can:

  • Feel like a dull pain, ache, 'heavy' or 'tight' feeling in your chest
  • Spread to your arms, neck, jaw or back
  • Be triggered by physical exertion

Diagnosing Angina

To diagnose angina, you will be assessed and you may need to have several tests. You will also be asked about:

  • The symptoms you experienced
  • If there were any triggers for the pain
  • Your family's medical history
  • Lifestyle risk factors (like smoking)

Tests for Angina

You might be given tests to determine if you have heart disease or you're at risk of it:

  • Measuring your weight and blood pressure
  • Blood tests to check cholesterol levels, diabetes or anaemia

Treating Angina

There are various ways to treat and manage angina.

Stable Angina Treatment

If you have stable angina you may be given medicine to:

  • Prevent and treat angina attacks
  • Reduce your risk of a heart attack

Unstable Angina

Worsening angina symptoms over a short period can be a sign of acute coronary syndrome. Seek urgent medical advice by phoning 999.

Surgery for Angina

Two main procedures are used to treat angina:

Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG)

A section of blood vessel is taken from another part of the body to re-route blood flow past blocked arteries.

Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI)

A narrowed artery is widened using a balloon and a tiny tube called a stent. Also known as coronary angioplasty.
