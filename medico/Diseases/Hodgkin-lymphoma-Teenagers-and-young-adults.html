Hodgkin Lymphoma: Teenagers and Young Adults

This section is for teenagers and young adults and is about a type of cancer called Hodgkin lymphoma.

Good news: Treatment for Hodgkin lymphoma has a very good success rate and most people are cured.

Hodgkin lymphoma is a cancer of the lymphatic system, which is one of the body's natural defences against infection.

Lymphomas, like all cancers, are a disease of the body's cells. Normally cells in our body divide and grow in a controlled way. But sometimes cells keep dividing and grow out of control. This is how cancer develops.

In lymphomas, white blood cells called lymphocytes become abnormal and grow out of control. These lymphocytes can build up in one part of the body and form a lump (tumour).

Types of Lymphoma

There are 2 main types of lymphoma:

  • Hodgkin lymphoma
  • Non-Hodgkin lymphoma (NHL)

Although these cancers are both lymphomas, they are different and need different treatments. Your doctors will do tests to find out which type you have.

Symptoms of Hodgkin Lymphoma

One of the most common symptoms is having a painless lump. The lump is caused by swollen lymph nodes. There may be one or more lumps and these may be in your neck, armpit or groin.

Lumps can also be inside the body. A common place would be inside the chest and this can cause a cough or breathlessness.

Other Common Symptoms

  • Feeling tired and lethargic
  • Itching of the skin
  • High temperatures (fevers)
  • Very heavy sweats (especially at night)
  • Unexplained weight loss

Doctors call symptoms like high temperatures, heavy sweats, and weight loss 'B symptoms'.

Important: Most of these symptoms may also be caused by other illnesses. If you have any of these symptoms or are worried that you may have Hodgkin lymphoma, see your GP. They'll examine you and refer you to a hospital if needed.

Causes of Hodgkin Lymphoma

It's not known what causes Hodgkin lymphoma. Research is going on to try to find out.

Remember: Hodgkin lymphoma isn't anything that you've done. It's not your fault.

Who Gets Hodgkin Lymphoma?

Hodgkin lymphoma is one of the most common cancers in young people over the age of 10. About 1 in 6 of all cancers affecting young people between 15 and 24 is a Hodgkin lymphoma.

Important Facts

  • Hodgkin lymphoma isn't infectious
  • It can't be passed on to other people or family members
  • It's not caused by anything you did or didn't do

If you're looking for information about Hodgkin lymphoma in people of all ages, please consult with healthcare professionals for comprehensive information.

Treatment and Prognosis

Positive Outlook: Hodgkin lymphoma has one of the highest cure rates among cancers, especially in young people.

Treatment Approaches

Treatment for Hodgkin lymphoma typically involves:

  • Chemotherapy - using drugs to kill cancer cells
  • Radiotherapy - using high-energy rays to target cancer cells
  • Targeted therapies - newer treatments that target specific aspects of cancer cells
  • In some cases, stem cell transplantation

What to Expect

Your treatment will be tailored to your specific situation, considering:

  • The stage of your lymphoma
  • Your age and overall health
  • The specific type of Hodgkin lymphoma
  • Any symptoms you're experiencing

Support is available: There are many support services for young people with cancer, including specialist nurses, counselors, and support groups who understand what you're going through.

Remember, you're not alone in this journey. There are healthcare professionals and support networks ready to help you through treatment and recovery.
